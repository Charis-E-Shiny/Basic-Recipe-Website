Gulab Jamun Recipe

Gulab Jamun Recipe - Gulab jamuns are soft delicious berry sized balls made with milk solids
flour & a leavening agent. These are soaked in rose flavored sugar syrup & enjoyed. The word "Gulab" translates to
rose in Hindi & "jamun" to berry. So, gulab jamun are berry sized balls dunked in rose flavored sugar syrup! ðŸŒ¹

How to Make Gulab Jamun

Ingrdients Required
  1. 125 g Khoya or mawa
  2. 2+1/2 tablespoons Maida
  3. 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  4. 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  5. 2 - 4 tbsp milk or water
  6. 2 tbsp paneer
  7. oil or ghee as needed for frying
  8. 1+1/4 -1+1/2 cups of sugar
  9. 3/4 cup water
  10. 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  11. 1 tsp rose water
  12. 1+1/2 tsp lemon juice

Instructions

Make sugar syrup

1. To a wide pot, add sugar and water. Heat on a medium flame until the sugar dissolves. Then let the
syrup boil until it reaches a sticky consistency.

2. Take 1/4 tsp of it, cool slightly. Take a little in between your thumb and forefinger. It should be
sticky when you seperate your fingers.

3. Add rose water, cardamom powder and lemon juice. Mix well and set it aside to keep the syrup slightly
hot.

Making Dough

1. In a mixing bowl, stir together grated mawa, add flour, cadamom powder and then baking powder.

2. Pour milk only as needed little by little to make a smooth crack-free dough. You can gently knead it
but don't over knead as it affects the texture and forms gluten. You must get a dough ball that is smooth
with not many cracks. It should not be dry or crumbly.

3. Divide the dough to 14-16 parts.

4. Take each balls in between your palms, rub it gently a few times until smooth and roll to a ball. The
balls should be a smooth and crack-free.

How to make gulab jamun with khoya

1. Heat oil in a deep pot or kadai.

2. Check if the oil is hot by gently sliding a small piece of dough. It must go in and rise slowly with
bubbles & should not brown. This is the right time to fry the balls.

3. Regulate the flame to medium. Begin to drop one ball at a time. Slowly add as many balls as possible.

4. After frying for a few minutes they will firm up. Keep stirring and fry them uniformly until golden
brown. If needed switch the stove flame to low and medium.

5 Remove the gulab jamuns to a colander. Continue frying and removing the fried ones to a colander.

6. Allow the balls to a cool a bit for 3 to 5 mins. Add them to slightly hot syrup. The syrup should not
be very hot. When you dip your finger you must feel the syrup is hot but not very hot.

7. Gently rotate the gulab jamuns in the syrup. Let them soak for 45 mins before serving.
