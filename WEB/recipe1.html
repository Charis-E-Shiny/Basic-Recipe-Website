Masala Dosa Recipe

Masala Dosa Recipe - Make perfect, aromatic and great masala dosas at home by following this
step by step recipe post

Dosa is a crepe made using fermented rice and lentil batter. Masala dosa is one that is crisp, aromatic,
flavourful and has potato masala or spiced seasoned potatoes stuffed in it.

How to Make Masala Dosa

Ingrdients Required
  1. 1/2 cup urad dal
  2. 3 tbsp channa dal
  3. 1/2 tsp mathi seeds
  4. 3 tbsp poha
  5. 1+1/2 cups raw rice
  6. 1/2 cups idli rice
    7. (optional)
  7. potaetoes
  8. 1 tsp channa
  9. 1 tsp urad
  10. 1/2 tsp cumin
  11. 3/4 tsp mustard seeds
  12. grated ginger
  13. sliced onions
  14. chopped green chillies
  15. curry leaves
  16. turmeric
  17. salt
  18. coriander leaves
  19. oil

Prepare and Make the Batter

1. Add half cup urad dal, 3 tablespoons channa da and half teaspoon methi seeds to a large pot or bowl.
Also measure 3 tablespoons poha and set aside. Wash and soak it just 30 mins before blending the batter.

2. Also measure 1+1/2 cups raw rice & 1/2 cups idli rice (optional). You can also use only raw rice. Add
them to another large bowl.

3. Wash and soak them seperately for 4 to 5 hours

4. Driain water from dal. Add dal and poha to a blender jar with 1+1/4 cup water and salt as needed. You
can skip salt if you live in a hot place. without adding salt, my batter doesn't ferment. So I use for
blending.

5. Blend them to a batter that is frothy and bubbly. The batter must not be very thick or runny at this
stage.

6. Tranfer that to a bowl. Use glass or ceramic bowls if you live in a hot place. This prevents the
batter from running sour.

7. Blend rice with half cup water to a slightly coarse (fine semolina texture) or a smooth batter to
suit your liking. If you have a small blender then blend the rice in 2 batches.

8. If needed pour 1/4 cup water.

9. Pour that as well to the bowl with dal batter.

10. Mix everything well

11. Cover and allow to ferment in a warm place for 6 to 16 hours depending on your climate or temperature.

Ferment Masala Dosa batter

12. Cover the batter & keep it in a warm place to ferment until the batter rises and turns slightly bubbly.
If you live in a warm place, leave it on the counter overnight. It may take anywhere from 5 to 16 hours
depending on the temperature.

Fermentation test: To check if the masala dosa batter is fermented well, drop half a spoon
of this batter into a bowl filled with water. Well fermented batter will float & not sink.

Make Potato Masala for Dosa

13. The next morning, steam and boil potatoes without making them mushy. You can steam them over a pressure
cooker. Or Add peeled and diced potaetoes to a bowl and pour water to partially cover them. Place it in the
the pressure cooker and let it whistle thrice. While the potaetoes boil, soak 1 tsp chana dal & 1 tsp urad
dal in little hot water for 30 mins so they soften. Drain the water and keep aside before using in the next step.

14. Heat a pan with 1 tbsp oil. Saute 1/2 tsp cumin, 3/4 tsp mustard seeds, tsp chana dal and 1 tsp urad dal.
When the dals turn golden, add hing.

15. Add grated ginger and saute until aroma comes out.

16. Add sliced onions, slit or chopped green chilies and curry leaves.

17. Saute until the onions turn lightly golden.

18. Crumble the potaetoes & add along with turmeric and salt.

19. Add 2 tbsps of potato steamed water or fresh water. If your potaetoes have turned mushy, just skip the
water here.

20. Saute very well for 2 mins and add chopped coriander leaves. Set this aside.

Make the Masala Dosa

21. Bring the masala dosa batter to the right consistency by adding some water. Batter must be a thick
pouring consistency.

22. Heat your pan on medium heat. If using cast iron pan, drizzle a few drops of oil and rub it all over
a cut onion. When the pan is hot enough, stir the batter in the bowl and take a laddle of batter. pour it
in the centre of the hot pan.

23. Immediately begin to spread the batter from the centre moveing your hand in clockwise direction,
making circles until you reach the edges.

24. When it turns slightly brown and well cooked. Flip it and cook on the other side. Flip it back and cook
cook it unitl the masala turns golden to light brown. Place the potato masala on one half of the dosa.

25. Either roll it or just fold it.
